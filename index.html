Our Story

A Living Legacy in the Hills of Haleri

Haleri was once the seat of Coorg's last sovereigns, the Haleri kings. In 1863, the English planter Frank Mangles brought coffee to these slopes — part of the story that made Coorg one of India's great coffee regions.

The Hallery's bungalows were planter residences, restored by our family from the 1960s and kept as a living, working estate. A place that has been tended, not built.
