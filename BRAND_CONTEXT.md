## 1. Brand Story

The Hallery sits in Haleri, the historic seat of Coorg's last royal dynasty (the Haleri/Paleri Kings). In 1863 the English planter Frank Mangles introduced European coffee cultivation to the estate — part of the wave that made Coorg one of India's most celebrated coffee regions. The bungalows were private planter residences, restored from the 1960s onward by the custodian family into an intimate heritage stay that still operates as a genuine working coffee-and-spice plantation.

The story to communicate is **continuity, not novelty**: royal legacy → colonial-era coffee heritage → a living, working estate today. It is a place that has been *kept*, not built. Two threads must always coexist: **heritage** (dynasty, Raj-era architecture, antiques) and **living nature** (a real plantation, forest, water, animals).

## 2. Brand Personality

- **Custodial** — a keeper of a place and its history, not a hotel operator.
- **Understated** — quiet confidence; never showy or loud.
- **Warm & hands-on** — personal, familial hospitality; guests participate in estate life.
- **Rooted** — deeply of its place (Coorg, coffee, Kodava culture), never generic.
- **Unhurried** — time slows here; the pace is the product.
- **Authentic** — a real working estate, not a themed facsimile.

Personality contrast for calibration: The Hallery is the more **hands-on, intimate, homely-heritage** sibling; Old Kent is the more polished/refined-luxury sibling. When in doubt, choose intimate and rooted over slick and grand.

## 3. Target Audience

- **Couples & honeymooners** — seeking romance, privacy, seclusion.
- **Nature & slow-travel seekers** — want immersion (plantation, forest, water, wildlife), not nightlife.
- **Heritage/culture-curious travellers** — drawn to the dynasty + coffee-history narrative.
- **Discerning urban professionals** — escaping noise; value authenticity and calm over flashy amenities.

They are choosing *escape, meaning, and place* — not a checklist of facilities. Design and content should reward stillness and curiosity, not urgency.

## 4. Luxury Positioning

- Luxury here = **privacy, authenticity, heritage, and nature** — NOT gold-plated opulence, nightlife, or scale.
- It is **understated / quiet luxury**: antiques, four-poster beds, vaulted wood ceilings, plantation views, personal service.
- The luxury is **experiential and immersive** (crop-to-cup coffee, private waterfall, candlelit dinners under the stars) rather than transactional.
- Intimacy IS the premium: ~8 rooms means exclusivity and seclusion, not volume.
- Never position on price, discounts, or urgency. Position on rarity of experience and depth of place.

## 5. Emotional Keywords

Use as a north star for copy, imagery, and interaction — **serene, tranquil, unhurried, rooted, heritage, nostalgic, warm, intimate, secluded, misty, verdant, grounded, restorative, timeless, soulful, authentic, quiet.**

Avoid the opposite register: energetic, buzzing, trendy, glamorous, opulent, urgent, exclusive-as-in-elitist.

## 6. Tone of Voice

- **Register:** calm, literary, sincere. Speaks like a gracious host with a long memory, not a sales team.
- **Sentences:** measured; let single ideas land. Sensory and concrete over abstract superlatives.
- **Person:** guest = "you"; brand = "we"/"The Hallery."
- **Do:** evoke place and heritage; name specifics (coffee, spice, mist, the Rosarium); respect the reader's intelligence.
- **Don't:** hard-sell, manufacture urgency, use clichés ("nestled," "world-class," "unforgettable"), exclamation marks, or emoji.
- Heritage references should feel **proud but not colonial-nostalgic in a naive way** — acknowledge the Raj-era architecture as history/aesthetic, keep the Kodava/Coorg identity central.

## 7. Brand Values

1. **Preservation** — of heritage, architecture, and a working agricultural way of life.
2. **Authenticity** — a real estate with real coffee, spice, and produce.
3. **Tranquility** — protecting quiet, space, and slowness as the core offering.
4. **Rootedness in place** — Coorg's land, culture, and biodiversity are central, not backdrop.
5. **Personal hospitality** — warm, familial, hands-on care at small scale.
6. **Stewardship of nature** — forest, water, bees, animals as part of the estate's life.

## 8. Unique Selling Points

- A **genuine 260-acre working coffee & spice estate** — not a resort dressed as one.
- **Royal + coffee heritage** in one place (Haleri Kings; Frank Mangles, 1863).
- **Intimacy** — ~8 rooms; seclusion and exclusivity by scale.
- **Every room overlooks coffee & spice plantations.**
- **On-property private waterfall** and forest trails.
- **Crop-to-cup coffee experience** on the land where Coorg coffee history began.
- **Working farm life** guests can touch: dairy farm, petting zoo, apiculture.
- **Coorgi & South Indian cuisine** in a **rose-garden setting (The English Rosarium)**.
- Part of a **trusted heritage-stay family** (with Old Kent Estate).

## 9. Estate Features

- 260 acres of coffee and spice plantation set in the Coorg mountains.
- Restored British-planter heritage architecture (bungalow + cottages).
- Private waterfall on the property.
- Forest and plantation trails; ponds / natural reservoir.
- Working dairy farm; petting zoo; apiary (beekeeping).
- The English Rosarium — a rose garden used as a dining/contemplative space.
- Spacious private cottages with balconies and plantation views; emphasis on privacy.

## 10. Coffee Plantation Experience

- The estate is a **living coffee & spice plantation**, not decorative.
- Signature experience: **guided plantation walks that explain coffee "from crop to cup"** — cultivation, harvesting, processing, tasting.
- Historical weight: this is near where **European coffee cultivation in Coorg began (1863)** — the coffee narrative is also a heritage narrative.
- Spice cultivation (e.g., pepper, cardamom, cinnamon — Coorg-typical) accompanies coffee; one cottage is named **Cinnamon**, reinforcing the spice identity.
- Design implication: coffee should be treated as *craft and heritage*, sensory and earthy — not as a café/commodity motif.

## 11. Heritage Story

- **Layer 1 — Royal:** Haleri was the seat of the Haleri/Paleri Kings, Coorg's last sovereign dynasty. The brand name and identity draw from this.
- **Layer 2 — Colonial coffee era:** Frank Mangles, 1863, European coffee cultivation; British-planter bungalows.
- **Layer 3 — Restoration & continuity:** family-restored from the 1960s; still a working estate; "living legacy."
- Present all three as one continuous thread of custodianship. Architecture and interiors (antiques, four-poster beds, seasonal-hued suites, vaulted wood ceilings) are the tangible evidence of this heritage.

## 12. Nature Experience

- Immersive, multi-sensory, and gentle: **mist, mountains, forest, water, birdsong, coffee blossom, rain.**
- Activities: forest wandering, plantation trails, **private waterfall**, pond/reservoir exploration, outdoor yoga, wildlife/biodiversity along guided walks.
- The mood is **restorative and contemplative**, not adventure-adrenaline. Nature here is for slowing down.
- Seasonality matters in Coorg (monsoon mist, harvest, blossom) — treat weather and season as atmosphere, not obstacle.

## 13. Room Categories

Anchor to the real, named accommodations (confirm exact counts/names with client before publishing):

- **Mangles Bungalow** — the heritage centerpiece; **four interconnected suites** in *seasonal hues*, with **antique furnishings and four-poster beds**. Named for Frank Mangles. Register: grand-but-homely heritage.
- **Rosarium** — a manager's cottage; **rustic elegance, vaulted wood ceilings**; tied to the rose-garden identity.
- **Cinnamon** — a manager's cottage; **rustic elegance, vaulted wood ceilings**; tied to the spice identity.
- **Common thread:** all accommodations **overlook coffee & spice plantations**; emphasis on privacy, balconies, antiques, heritage character. Total scale ~8 rooms.
- Do NOT invent generic tiers ("Deluxe / Superior / Executive"). The rooms are *named, characterful heritage spaces* — that individuality is the point.

## 14. Experiences Offered

- Guided coffee & spice **plantation walks** (crop to cup).
- **Private waterfall** visit; forest & nature trails; pond/reservoir exploration.
- **Working dairy farm** visit; **petting zoo** (feeding animals); **apiculture** (beekeeping).
- **Outdoor yoga** and relaxation.
- **For couples:** private **candlelight dinners under the stars**, **bonfire** evenings, **curated plantation picnics**, thoughtful **turndown service**.
- Framing: experiences are *curated, personal, and estate-rooted* — arranged by hosts, small-scale, unhurried.

## 15. Dining Experience

- Cuisine: **Coorgi (Kodava) and South Indian**, described as *fresh, nourishing, soul-warming* — farm-to-table, estate-sourced sensibility.
- Signature setting: **The English Rosarium** — a rose garden framed as a sanctuary (poetry, love, nature); use for atmospheric, romantic dining moments.
- Register: homely, seasonal, ingredient-led, personal — NOT fine-dining-restaurant formality. Do not invent star ratings or celebrity-chef narratives.
- Romance is a strong dining theme (candlelight dinners under the stars) given the honeymoon audience.

## 16. Why Guests Choose The Hallery

- To **disconnect and slow down** in genuine seclusion.
- For **authentic heritage + working-estate immersion**, not a generic resort.
- For **privacy and intimacy** (few rooms, plantation-only views).
- For **romance** (honeymoons, anniversaries; couple-focused experiences).
- For **nature immersion** (waterfall, forest, mist, farm life).
- For the **coffee-and-heritage story** — a place with real history and real craft.
- For **warm, personal hospitality** at small scale.

## 17. Visual Inspiration

- **Reference worlds:** heritage plantation bungalows of the Western Ghats; British-Raj colonial interiors (antiques, four-posters, wood); the misted green of Coorg; Kodava culture; National-Geographic-style editorial nature photography; quiet-luxury heritage-hotel design (Aman/heritage-Taj register, but homelier).
- **Palette:** deep plantation greens, misty greys/whites, earthy browns and coffee tones, warm wood, muted golds, terracotta/rose accents (the Rosarium). Natural, muted, earthy — never neon or high-saturation.
- **Textures:** raw wood, stone, foliage, coffee cherries/beans, linen, aged brass, rose petals, mist.
- **Composition:** generous negative space, natural framing, editorial and unhurried. Heritage detail shots + expansive landscape both matter.
- **Avoid:** stocky "smiling staff" hotel imagery, high-gloss corporate resort aesthetics, template card grids, saturated travel-brochure looks.

## 18. Photography Style

- **Natural light**, atmospheric — early-morning mist, golden hour, soft overcast, monsoon greens.
- **Full-bleed, editorial** landscapes (plantation rows, mountains, waterfall) balanced with **intimate detail** (coffee cherries, a four-poster, brass, rose petals, a cup of estate coffee).
- Muted, true-to-nature color grading; earthy and green-dominant; gentle contrast; no heavy filters.
- Show **place and craft over product**; people, when present, are candid and immersed (a walk, a picnic), never posed hotel-brochure smiles.
- Sense of **scale and seclusion** — wide shots that convey space and quiet.

## 19. Video Style

- **Slow, cinematic, ambient.** Long takes, gentle movement, real estate sounds (birdsong, rain, rustling coffee leaves, distant water) over minimal or acoustic score.
- Content: drifting mist over plantations, the waterfall, dawn over the hills, coffee processing, a candlelit dinner, farm animals, hands at work.
- Grade to match photography — natural, earthy, green. No fast cuts, no high-energy montage, no aggressive music.
- Goal: make the viewer **exhale** — convey calm, space, and immersion, not excitement.

## 20. Motion Style

- **Slow, soft, purposeful** (aligns with PROJECT_RULES.md §6). Gentle fades and small parallax that echo drifting mist; nothing bouncy, snappy, or attention-grabbing.
- Motion should feel like *breathing / slow reveal*, mirroring the unhurried pace of the estate.
- Scroll should feel like walking through the estate — each section a considered step in a story (heritage → estate → coffee → nature → rooms → experiences → dining → arrival).
- Respect reduced-motion always. Motion supports mood; it never performs.
